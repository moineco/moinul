Biography

1 Background

I am Dr. Moinul Islam, serving as an Associate Professor at Kochi University of Technology, Japan. I earned my Doctor of Engineering (D.Eng.) [equivalent to Ph.D. in Environmental Engineering] degree from Kyushu University in 2016. My expertise spans agriculture, climate, ecological, environmental, and behavioral economics, with a particular interest in field experiments. My research focuses on applying economic models to address critical public policy challenges. I am engaged in understanding the far-reaching impacts of climate change and natural disasters on human life and finding solutions through experimental evidence-based public policies.

I have extensive experience in designing laboratory, field, and survey experiments, as well as in collecting and analyzing primary data to address my research questions. I am also proficient in analyzing secondary data, including cross-sectional, time series, spatial, and panel data by employing advanced econometric techniques to derive robust insights for my research. For over a decade, I have been actively researching climate change adaptation and public policy, particularly in developing countries to protect climate vulnerable populations.

3 Research interests

4 My vision

A future where human behavior aligns with nature to foster resilience against climate change and promote sustainable development.

5 My mission

Identify human behavioral gaps between current and ideal societal systems to formulate research questions (RQ) at the intersection of economics, environmental systems, and public policy, and investigate them through rigorous economic and experimental methods aimed at fostering sustainable human–nature coexistence.

6 My strategies

Pursue applied economics research that integrates environmental, behavioral, and policy perspectives to address pressing sustainability issues.

Foster collaboration with scholars in sustainability science and social science to develop holistic solutions to complex environmental and societal challenges.

Engage with local governments and communities to co-produce context-sensitive social experiments that informs globally relevant and locally actionable sustainability policies.
